# Welcome to Operion

Operion is your operations hub. From one place you can take customer bookings,
send crews out on jobs, keep track of the money, pay your team, and handle the
occasional damage claim. This guide walks you through the everyday tasks in plain
language — no technical background needed.

> **Tip.** Everything in Operion is designed around two ideas: a **booking** (a
> customer’s job) and a **route** (the crew assignment that gets it done). Keep
> those two in mind and the rest falls into place.

---

# 1 Signing in

1. Open the admin site in your browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge, or Firefox).
2. **Owners:** leave the email field blank and enter the owner password.
3. **Everyone else:** enter your email and password.
4. Crew members are taken to the **Crew Portal**; admins and managers land on the
   **Operations** home.

> **Note.** For your security you’re signed out after **10 minutes** of inactivity
> (and after 2 hours no matter what). Just sign back in. Forgot your password? Ask
> an administrator to set a new one for you — there’s no self-service reset.

---

# 2 Finding your way around

The dark navigation bar is your map.

- **On a computer:** the top bar shows your main sections, a **More** menu for
  everything else, a **search** button, and a **Book Now bell** that shows how many
  online requests are waiting.
- **On a phone:** a bar across the bottom holds your main sections with a big
  **Book Now (+)** button in the middle, and a **More** sheet for the rest.
- **Press ⌘K (Ctrl+K)** anywhere to open the **command palette** and jump straight
  to any page — it’s the fastest way to get around.
- The floating **“+” button** always starts a **New assignment**.

| Section | What it’s for |
|---|---|
| Home | Your daily snapshot (see §3) |
| Schedule | Everything happening, by day or week |
| Operations | Your crew assignments (routes) |
| Messages | Talk to your crew and see reminders |
| Crew | Your team roster and pay |
| Book Now | Online customer requests waiting for you |
| More → Businesses, Equipment, Claims, Pay, Communications, Settings | Everything else |

> **Tip.** A red badge or chip always means “this needs attention.”

---

# 3 Your daily dashboard (Home)

Home gives you the pulse of the day at a glance:

- **Needs confirmation / Needs reassignment / Tomorrow** — quick counts that click
  through to the exact list.
- **Money** — a shortcut to your revenue and profit view.
- **Claims** — a quiet summary that only appears when there’s something open.
- **Book Now Requests** — how many online submissions are in, broken down by stage,
  each tile clicking straight to that group.
- **Today’s / Tomorrow’s operations** — expandable cards for each job with the crew,
  report time, address (tap to navigate), and instructions.

> **Tip.** After 5 pm, Home automatically switches its focus list to **tomorrow** so
> you can get a head start.

# 4 Taking a booking (Book Now)

When a customer requests a quote online, Operion helps you price it fast.

**How it flows:**

1. The customer picks a service, answers a few questions, and uploads photos.
2. For junk-removal-style jobs, Operion’s AI looks at the photos and suggests a
   size and a price range. **The AI only makes observations — your pricing rules
   always set the final number.**
3. Clear, simple jobs get an **instant quote**. Anything uncertain — possible
   hazardous materials, very large loads, or blurry photos — is sent to **Manual
   Review** for you to price by hand.
4. Everything lands in your **Book Now** queue, grouped by stage. You’ll also get a
   text and email when a new request arrives.

**Your job:** work the **Book Now** queue each day. Clear the **Manual Review**
items first, then send out the **Quote Ready** ones.

> **Note.** If the AI can’t read the photos or hits a snag, the request is never
> lost — it’s simply held for you to handle. Moving and delivery jobs are always
> priced by hand, not by AI.

# 5 Bookings & customers

Open **Bookings** (under *More*) to manage customer jobs.

- **Search** by name, phone, email, or booking/invoice number.
- **Filter** by status (active, unpaid, unscheduled, completed, and more), and
  toggle **Archived** or **Test** items.
- Open any booking to see and edit the customer’s details, add **internal notes**
  (never shown to the customer), attach photos, and review the full history of
  messages and status changes.
- **Export** the current list to a spreadsheet, or **archive** several at once.

> **Note.** There isn’t a separate “customer file.” A customer’s information lives on
> their booking, and the **Customers** tab gives you a quick read-only rollup of who
> has booked, how often, and how much they’ve paid.

# 6 Dispatching a crew

Jobs get done by assigning crew to a **route** (an operation).

**Create a route:** press **“+” → New assignment** and follow the steps — choose the
business, set the price and equipment, enter the address, pick the date/time
(one-off or recurring), then add crew and their pay.

**Run the route (open it from Operations):**

1. **Assign** crew, then **Send** them the assignment text.
2. Crew **confirm** (from a text link or the portal). The route’s status updates
   itself as they respond.
3. On the day, **Mark complete** — or **No show** / **Cancel** if plans change.
4. From a completed route you can **file a claim** if something was damaged.

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Draft | No crew assigned yet |
| Assigned / Awaiting confirm | Crew added / texted, waiting on them |
| Confirmed | Everyone’s in |
| Declined / No response | Needs your attention |
| Completed / No show / Cancelled | Done (final) |

> **Tip.** The **Operations** list has an **Attention** filter that surfaces declined,
> unanswered, or short-staffed routes — check it first thing each morning.

# 7 The schedule

**Schedule** shows everything on one board — online bookings and crew routes
together — by **Day** or **Week**, plus **Pending** and **Unscheduled** views. It
flags **conflicts** (the same crew, truck, or time booked twice) so you can catch
them early.

> **Note.** The schedule is for *seeing* your day. To change a job, click its card to
> open the booking or route and edit it there — there’s no drag-and-drop.

**Availability** (under *More* / Settings) controls how many online jobs you accept
per day, your online deposit, and any closed/blackout days.

# 8 Money: getting paid & paying your crew

| Where | What you do |
|---|---|
| **Finance / “Money”** | See revenue in, payouts out, and estimated profit — filter by date, business, or crew |
| **Contractor Pay** | Group completed routes by contractor for a pay period; see net owed after any claim deductions |
| **Pay Statements** | Generate, print, email, or void a crew member’s statement; review pay-correction requests |
| **Client Invoices** | Bill business clients from completed routes; send a payment link (customers can pay online) |
| **Analytics** | Revenue trends, outstanding balances, and revenue by service, city, or ZIP |

> **Tip.** Statements and invoices print to PDF from your browser (⌘P → Save as PDF),
> and can be emailed to crew and clients directly.

# 9 Handling claims

**Claims** (under *More*) tracks damage and cost recovery.

1. **Open a claim** — from the Claims page, a business, or a completed route (which
   snapshots the job details automatically). Each claim gets its own number.
2. **Assign responsibility** — split the amount across the crew involved (equally,
   by percent, or by dollar).
3. **Recover** — start a **weekly deduction** (automatically capped at what the
   contractor actually earned that week), record a cash **payment**, **waive** a
   balance, or **adjust** with a reason.
4. **Close** the claim when it’s settled.

Crew are texted about claims that affect them, and everything is logged.

> **Warning.** Closing a claim with money still owed, or deleting a claim that
> already recovered money, asks you to confirm — deleting erases the record of why a
> contractor’s pay was reduced.

# 10 Messages & notifications

- **Messages** is your two-way thread with the crew (dispatch, reminders, replies).
- **Communications** (under *More*) shows channel health, lets you preview message
  templates, send a test, and review recent/failed messages.
- Customers automatically get texts and emails for confirmations, reminders,
  completion, and payment. Crew get assignment texts plus optional **confirmation**
  and **morning-of** reminders (both on by default — toggle them in Settings).
- You (the owner) get alerted about new submissions, customer replies, and other
  important events.

> **Note.** Text and email only go out when the messaging providers are configured.
> There is no mobile push app.

---

# 11 Settings you’ll actually use

Open **Settings** (admins only) to:

- Turn your **owner text/email alerts** on or off and set where they go.
- Turn crew **confirmation** and **morning-of** reminders on or off.
- Choose whether crew see their **pay amount**.

Other useful configuration lives nearby: **Availability** (online capacity &
deposit), **Disposal pricing**, **Equipment**, **Promo codes**, and your
**Cancellation & Refund policy** (which keeps a version history).

> **Tip.** Managers can run daily operations but can’t change settings, manage
> logins, or configure pay — those are admin-only.

---

# 12 Your team’s Crew Portal

Your crew get their own simplified app at the portal. They sign in with their email
and password to:

- See **today’s jobs** and one-tap **acknowledge** them.
- **Clock in/out** (with location) on confirmed routes.
- View **routes**, **pay** and statements, **documents**, and **messages**.
- Submit **availability** and **time-off** requests.

Crew only ever see their own information.

# 13 Releases (owner only)

The **Release Center** lets the owner review a build and, when needed, publish an
update or roll back to the previous version — always with a typed confirmation so it
can’t happen by accident.

- **System Details** shows the current build and settings (safe to view).
- **Publishing** and **rolling back** require the owner to type an exact confirmation
  phrase, and only take effect in the live production environment.

> **Warning.** Publish and rollback are the only actions that change your live
> website. They’re owner-only, require a typed phrase, and are safe to *review* at
> any time — nothing changes just by looking.

# 13 Tips & troubleshooting

| If you see… | It means… | Do this |
|---|---|---|
| A booking stuck in **Manual Review** | The AI wasn’t confident enough to auto-quote | Price it by hand from the Book Now queue |
| **“Admins only”** | Your account can’t open that page | Ask an administrator, or sign in with an admin account |
| Signed out unexpectedly | 10-minute inactivity timeout | Sign back in |
| Can’t **Mark complete** | The route isn’t **Confirmed** yet | Confirm the crew first |
| No texts/emails going out | Messaging isn’t fully configured | Check with your administrator |
| A page asks you to “set up Redis/Upstash” | Analytics storage isn’t configured | Ask your administrator |

**Daily rhythm that works well:** clear the **Book Now** queue → work the
**Attention** filter in Operations → confirm crew for today and tomorrow.
**Weekly:** run **Pay** and send **Statements**, review **Time Off** and
**Availability**, check open **Claims**, and send **Invoices**.
